# Knowing Where You're Going

Once AI knows your goals, it can help you compute, directionally, how to get to State Z. That sentence sounds simple but it points to something profound. AI is actually very good at helping you think through paths and strategies, at identifying resources and patterns and obstacles. But it can only do that work if you have given it something real to aim at. A vague goal produces vague guidance.

State Z is your destination. Not just a wish list, but a genuine, specific picture of where you want to be: what your life looks like, what your business is doing, what relationships you are in, what your finances look like, what kind of work you are doing each day. The more concrete you can make this, the more useful AI becomes as a partner in getting there. And I want to name something about this that I think often gets skipped: knowing where you are destined to go requires genuine reflection. Just going with the flow unconsciously is not the same thing. Drift is not a destination.

Imagination is actually pretty correlated with a self-directed life. The people who end up where they intended to be are almost always the people who could picture it first. This is becoming more true, not less, as the tools available to self-directed individuals get more powerful. Whether people like it or not, we are moving into a world where self-direction is increasingly necessary. The people who can imagine their State Z, hold it clearly, and orient their daily actions toward it are the ones who are going to have agency in this environment.

This is also, for me, a faith question. To know where you are going means being willing to ask: what am I made for? What has been placed in me that wants to come out? Those questions are not soft or secondary. They are the most important inputs you can give both yourself and any AI system working on your behalf.

## Key Takeaway

State Z is not a wish; it is a specific, imagined destination that you choose with intentionality, because without a clear destination, even the best tools cannot help you get there.
